What can Lustro do for me?

Lustro - Polish for mirror - “mirrors” all your Address Book contacts to a bunch of different formats or services:

  • CSV or Comma Separated Values, a way to store tabular data. Lustro creates one file with a contact per line and a comma separates the different details per contact. This format dates back to the early days of business computing making it a common file format on all computer platforms.
  • Tab delimited, looks like CSV but uses tabs to separate the contact details.
  • hCard or HTML vCard is a microformat for publishing your contacts details in HTML. Lustro creates a single XHTML file with all your contacts as hCards. This format may be useful to publish contact details on websites in a semantic way.
  • Google Contacts, overwrites all your Google Contacts with the ones in your Address Book. You can now use your Address Book contacts in any Google Contacts enabled (web)application. Probably the best example here is GMail.

The file exports (CSV, Tab delimited and hCard) will be saved to your desktop. The Google export will be exported to Google directly off course.

Address Book supports a few export formats out of the box where you don't need Lusto:

  • vCard a standard file format for electronic business cards compatible with most address book applications and e-mail clients. Address Book can create a vCard for a single person or a single vCard with multiple contacts.
  • Address Book Archive exports all your contacts to a single file that can be imported in Address Book. This seems the best way to make a quick backup from all you contacts.

Address Book also synchronizes with MobileMe, Exchange, Yahoo! and Google if you enable this in the preference pane. Lustro still includes the Google Contacts export as the Address Book syncing only works if you have connected an iPhone or iPod Touch before and the syncing mechanism is not perfect at the time of writing (e.g. company cards show an empty name in the GMail contacts list).
